## Legend

- Required character beats: minimum emotional/action beats that must appear in chapter draft
- Stakes progression: how pressure shifts from chapter start to chapter end

## Chapter 1 — Harry finds home in transit

POV: Harry | Act I

- Setup
  - Arthur, Molly, and all the children finish the logistics of getting Harry from the Dursleys to the Weasleys' American portkey route.
  - Harry expects cautious formality and distance, while the family is loud, affectionate, and improvisational.
  - The Haight House is introduced as a loving but crowded, imperfect base of operations.
- Escalation
  - Harry is drawn into the trip's family rhythm: frantic unpacking, Molly feeding everyone, and Arthur marvelling at Muggle objects.
  - The contrast between Harry's Dursley deprivation and the family's chaotic warmth becomes a central emotional pressure.
- Complication
  - In the late-evening settling, Harry notices a faint wrongness in the house's old closet/attic-adjacent space, subtle enough to be dismissed but too consistent to ignore.
  - Harry is torn between enjoying family comfort and staying on alert, creating an internal split.
- Mini-Resolution
  - By night, Harry allows himself to sleep among Weasleys for the first time without performing self-protection.
  - The emotional anchor is set: he has arrived somewhere that might actually hold him.
- Required character beats
  - Harry: first real belonging against ingrained hypervigilance.
  - Molly: over-caring maternal instinct without smothering.
  - Arthur: childlike joy at a mundane trip.
  - Ron: eager but awkward inclusion as new family brother.
- Stakes progression
  - Start: emotional displacement and isolation.
  - End: baseline safety and attachment are won; mystery pressure introduced at low level.

## Chapter 2 — The Haight House settles into motion

POV: Harry | Act I

- Setup
  - Morning in the Haight House as the family establishes routines around food, chores, and Muggle errands.
  - Arthur performs his first Muggle-focused amazement sequence (laundromat/parking meter/sourdough) while the twins prepare gadget-like experiments.
- Escalation
  - Fred and George trigger one of their first harmless but chaotic pranks and involve Harry by accident.
  - The house itself becomes a stage for both comic energy and magical normalcy in a Muggle environment.
- Complication
  - A routine enchantment tied to the family’s improvised fix begins to misbehave, suggesting an external irregularity touching household magic.
  - Fred and George's laughter is suddenly paired with adult concern for the first time.
- Mini-Resolution
  - The family converts the anomaly into a solvable household issue and re-centres itself through teamwork, still keeping the day playful.
  - Harry sees danger can be investigated without retreating from joy.
- Required character beats
  - Fred/George: mischief then resourceful responsibility.
  - Harry: willingness to participate in safe family mischief.
  - Arthur: joy collides with unexpected prudence.
  - Molly: control without panic.
- Stakes progression
  - Start: domestic warmth with low external stakes.
  - End: first local magical irregularity enters the plot; stakes rise to household-level uncertainty.

## Chapter 3 — Belonging through the city

POV: Harry | Act I

- Setup
  - Family sightseeing begins: cable cars, Fisherman's Wharf, bridge fog, and public astonishment.
  - Ron arrives with lingering embarrassment about money and manners.
- Escalation
  - San Francisco life blurs Hogwarts anxieties: class markers and inherited stigma lose power in the Weasleys' new setting.
  - Ron's internal comparison to past slights begins to crack.
- Complication
  - Amid sightseeing, a repeating symbolic fog pattern appears briefly at the edge of Ron's and Harry's sightlines, but is not immediately interpretable.
  - The family momentarily treats it as weather coincidence.
- Mini-Resolution
  - Ron gains a small but real increase in confidence; family banter becomes less defensive.
  - Harry shares in that emotional thaw, deepening his sense of ordinary life.
- Required character beats
  - Ron: begins to replace shame with cautious pride.
  - Harry: observes family-integration through city texture.
  - Arthur and Molly: keep family unified while touring.
  - Ginny: notices small magical oddities in public.
- Stakes progression
  - Start: character-integration beats dominate.
  - End: atmospheric anomaly introduced; stakes add a mystery layer tied to place.

## Chapter 4 — Fog Hollow threshold

POV: Harry | Act I

- Setup
  - The family seeks out Fog Hollow through the Chinatown access route.
  - Marisol and Gus are introduced as local anchors of the hidden quarter.
- Escalation
  - Fog Hollow appears both welcoming and alive with local rules, exposing contrasts between British and American wizarding culture.
  - The family tests itself socially through Pacifica Wands & Curios, local etiquette, and the Sourdough Cauldron.
- Complication
  - Access systems show unusual resistance: an alley sequence locks and the group must be vouched through a counter-ward mechanism.
  - The presence of defensive pressure hints at an active disturbance, not just historical oddity.
- Mini-Resolution
  - The trio secures temporary trust and legal safe passage with clear social obligations for future visits.
  - The magical community is now a living setting, not merely background.
- Required character beats
  - Marisol: confident local guide with playful, grounded authority.
  - Gus: quiet guardian with historical memory.
  - Harry: first emotional shift from observer to belonging participant.
  - Arthur: respectful curiosity without condescension.
- Stakes progression
  - Start: exploratory excitement and social grounding.
  - End: hidden-world access granted, and external risk is now concrete.

## Chapter 5 — Culture, classes, and patterning

POV: Ginny | Act I

- Setup
  - Coit Academy visit reveals academic and cultural differences: first class with Muggle Integration expectations.
  - Ginny arrives eager and perceptive, sketchbook in hand.
- Escalation
  - Ginny, already tracking odd symbols in public spaces, sketches a repeating motif tied to local ward design language.
  - Bill and Hermione-adjacent precision in observation from the children catches adult attention.
- Complication
  - Ginny notices that two unrelated sightings share one sigil logic, linking the symbol to spaces with stronger residual energy.
  - Adults still treat it as teenage curiosity, so Ginny is pushed to persist without validation.
- Mini-Resolution
  - Ginny secures a quiet moment to share the sketch with trusted readers, moving from private fear to collaborative intelligence.
  - Her line from playful observer to clue-bearer is locked in.
- Required character beats
  - Ginny: active detection and disciplined fear management.
  - Bill: early pattern recognition and protective mentorship.
  - Ginny/Marisol dynamic: local-to-local peering across educational culture.
  - Percy: social friction from etiquette versus lived community.
- Stakes progression
  - Start: educational contrast and family texture.
  - End: symbolic-mystery thread is now active; stakes shift to evidence gathering.

## Chapter 6 — Alcatraz resonance

POV: Harry | Act I/II bridge

- Setup
  - The family joins a historical Alcatraz tour, positioning the old magical wing as scenic backdrop.
  - Harry feels subtly disoriented by old dark traces.
- Escalation
  - Bill applies practical detection discipline and identifies that warding around the decommissioned wing is not merely aged—it's being pressured from outside.
  - Harry's instinctive dread aligns with Bill's technical finding.
- Complication
  - The apparent historical exhibit becomes a live forensic scene: tide markers and residue suggest active attempts to reopen sealed pathways.
  - A directional magical response points to the Underlook approaches.
- Mini-Resolution
  - The family moves from tourist mode to active concern, establishing that this is a threat route rather than a static oddity.
  - Shared agreement to investigate quietly before alarming broader communities.
- Required character beats
  - Harry: sensory magic instincts converted into concrete concern.
  - Bill: analytical authority; non-theatrical leadership.
  - Ginny and Ron: support the inference chain.
  - Arthur: begins noticing how little time is left before public damage.
- Stakes progression
  - Start: mild supernatural anomaly.
  - End: concrete threat vector identified; stakes become life-and-community scale.

## Chapter 7 — Night reconnaissance comedy

POV: Harry | Act II

- Setup
  - Fred and George run an off-hours Fog Hollow prank mission with Marisol as navigator.
  - The outing starts as familiar Weasley mischief with local flavor.
- Escalation
  - They stumble onto signs of surveillance and unusual cargo movement near the waterfront.
  - The joke lens turns investigative as the twins become accidental intelligence agents.
- Complication
  - They are observed by an unknown counter-intelligence method; the pelican (Barnacle) pattern marks premeditated tracking behavior.
  - Immediate decision: retreat quietly or pursue with improvised proof.
- Mini-Resolution
  - The group escapes immediate discovery while retaining at least one valuable clue of enemy mobility.
  - Harry, Ron, and Marisol's trust triangle strengthens under pressure.
- Required character beats
  - Fred/George: initiative, improvisation, and risk accountability.
  - Marisol: tactical mentorship and local knowledge.
  - Harry: transitions from participant to witness witness.
  - Ron: supports escape planning with calm tactical thinking.
- Stakes progression
  - Start: lighthearted reconnaissance.
  - End: direct antagonistic surveillance confirmed; stakes become tactical and urgent.

- Required character beats

  - Gus: intergenerational wisdom and warning.
  - Tomo: disciplined skepticism hardening into trust.
  - Bill: professional competence and restraint.
- Stakes progression
  - Start: alliance-building uncertainty.
  - End: formalized threat response team; stakes rise from local mystery to coordinated civic danger.

## Chapter 9 — Pattern converges

POV: Ron | Act II bridge

- Setup
  - Ginny's symbol work and Harry's sensory readings are brought into one workspace.
  - Ron undertakes a full board-level reasoning pass on disturbances and movement data.
- Escalation
  - Disturbance points converge into one locus in the Underlook system, revealing the true target geometry.
  - The family has enough intelligence for action, which means they are now visible to the enemy.
- Complication
  - As Ron finalizes his convergence map, an ominous marker appears tied to children's safety, confirming Voss's confidence in predictive pressure.
  - Strategic pressure increases because the enemy now has a probable map of their investigative priorities.
- Mini-Resolution
  - The team shifts from scattered detection to decisive operational planning, including who goes where and why.
  - Stakes are narrowed to a defendable and attackable objective.
- Required character beats
  - Ron: intellectual leadership without showmanship.
  - Harry: trusts Ron's pattern model and joins action.
  - Ginny: keeps symbol interpretation anchored to practical clues.
  - Bill: translates map into tactical next steps.
- Stakes progression
  - Start: data scattered across characters.
  - End: single high-value target identified; stakes become immediate and adversarial.

## Chapter 10 — Underlook first contact

POV: Harry | Act II

- Setup
  - Bill and Tomo stage a controlled perimeter walk into the Underlook's outer tunnels.
  - The party prepares for minimal exposure and possible counter-measures.
- Escalation
  - They confirm ritual components and timing signals, including anchor points and transport routes.
  - The investigators move from hypothesis to proof.
- Complication
  - Harlan's surveillance net activates: maintenance routes shift, egress points are narrowed, and there is fresh compulsion residue at an exit lip.
  - The pair must leave with only partial physical proof before being cornered into compromise.
- Mini-Resolution
  - They extract before full contact with the full hostile team and preserve witness-level evidence.
  - Alarm status elevates and all involved families are moved into crisis posture.
- Required character beats
  - Bill: disciplined exit under pressure.
  - Tomo: field leadership in constrained spaces.
  - Harry and Arthur: receive threat in adult briefing and commit.
  - Ron: begins operational logistics for contingencies.
- Stakes progression
  - Start: high-value reconnaissance.
  - End: operational war game starts; stakes are now pursuit and imminent encounter.

## Chapter 11 — Compulsion test and family fracture

POV: Arthur | Act III

- Setup
  - Fog Hollow remains socially active while adults monitor signs of escalation.
  - Arthur and the family debate containment versus engagement.
- Escalation
  - Voss tests compulsion fog in public corridors, producing confusing memory gaps and emotional panic among residents.
  - Arthur and Molly each defend different priorities: community duty vs immediate family safety.
- Complication
  - Personal networks are affected directly, including people the family trusts, forcing the ethics decision into a real dilemma.
  - The children become dangerously uncontainable under adult directives, and the adults lose informational control.
- Mini-Resolution
  - The family lands on a hard compromise: adults maintain control of direct action; children are warned but not fully contained.
  - Arthur and Molly both choose not to abandon one another, preserving unit cohesion for the next phase.
- Required character beats
  - Arthur: leadership strain and reluctant acceptance of danger.
  - Molly: protective ferocity and strategic restraint.
  - Harry: moral tension balancing obedience and concern.
  - Children group: begins acting independently but still tethered.
- Stakes progression
  - Start: strategic uncertainty and community anxiety.
  - End: moral stakes crystallize around children at risk and family unity.

## Chapter 12 — Children cross the line

POV: Harry | Act III

- Setup
  - Despite adult warning, the children agree to investigate the Underlook with Marisol, convinced the adults are missing timing.
  - They prepare improvised, child-scale reconnaissance.
- Escalation
  - Harlan and the hostile team intercept at a deeper layer than expected, proving the caves are operational and trap-laden.
  - Escape now requires fast tactical adaptation, not merely bravery.
- Complication
  - The team is cornered in constrained tunnel geometry and one member is almost separated from group support.
  - A full fight risk appears, but the children cannot afford fatal outcomes.
- Mini-Resolution
  - Fred and George's joke mechanics, Ron's planning, and Harry's instinctive magic create a narrow escape path.
  - Isolde's refusal to trigger a lethal variant of her craft creates the first real soft victory.
- Required character beats
  - Harry: instinctive protective leadership among peers.
  - Ron: tactical clarity under stress.
  - Fred/George: playful tools become survival assets.
  - Ginny: evidence recall and emotional discipline.
  - Isolde: first true moral crack with consequence.
- Stakes progression
  - Start: unauthorized youth initiative.
  - End: immediate survival crisis and widened vulnerability; stakes jump to near-catastrophic for child characters.

## Chapter 13 — Final convergence

POV: Harry | Act IV

- Setup
  - Team reunites and consolidates all earlier clues and losses.
  - Tomo relays timing and anchor status; Isolde's potential defection is assessed in tactical terms.
- Escalation
  - Voss has already primed Anchor One; the ritual can shift into irreversible mode on short notice.
  - Arthur, Bill, and the children coordinate who covers which corridor and anchor.
- Complication
  - The ritual fog begins early-stage behavior changes before the team physically occupies a full plan position.
  - Decision point: immediate assault versus delayed synchronized strike.
- Mini-Resolution
  - The group accepts the risk and moves in synchronized teams, trading comfort for momentum.
  - Operational plan is now committed and no retreat plan is clean.
- Required character beats
  - Arthur: decisive commander under fatherly burden.
  - Bill: integrates tactical and ward-level expertise.
  - Ron and Ginny: maintain mapping support and civilian safety roles.
  - Marisol: local route expertise with emotional loyalty.
- Stakes progression
  - Start: strategic planning and moral burden.
  - End: no-escape endgame; stakes become terminal if execution fails.

## Chapter 14 — Ritual collapse sequence

POV: Harry | Act IV

- Setup
  - Team executes entry and disruption sequence near multiple ward anchors.
  - Children are placed in defensible positions and contingency lines are set.
- Escalation
  - The ritual destabilizes violently; fog-compulsion conditions intensify by the minute.
  - Harlan intervenes physically while Arthur and Bill work simultaneous technical tasks.
- Complication
  - A split is forced: Arthur must secure child safety while Bill and Tomo attempt final anchor control under hostile pressure.
  - Voss pushes compulsion directly at Harry at peak stress.
- Mini-Resolution
  - Molly's defensive shell protects the children while the adults force the ritual anchors offline.
  - Isolde blocks completion of the final lethal step, and Harlan is neutralized by the combined efforts of Fred, George, and Marisol.
- Required character beats
  - Arthur: disciplined duel and prioritization under chaos.
  - Bill: precise spellwork under interference.
  - Tomo: field execution under time compression.
  - Molly: fierce protection instinct.
  - Fred/George/Marisol: coordinated action from earlier rivalry/banter into teamwork.
- Stakes progression
  - Start: multi-front battle at peak complexity.
  - End: victory path opens but is not yet secure; physical threat imminent from collapsed wards.

## Chapter 15 — Homeward seal

POV: Harry | Act IV

- Setup
  - Voss makes final compulsion attempt on Harry as the ritual core nears collapse.
  - The remaining wards are unstable and flood pressure builds through old channels.
- Escalation
  - Harry resists despite youth and shock, showing a hard-won instinctive refusal.
  - The team executes emergency tide and ward exits while preventing mass panic.
- Complication
  - Timing windows are narrow: one mistake means either flood loss or uncontrolled fog compulsion release.
  - Molly and Arthur must choose personal rescue risk over clean tactical withdrawal.
- Mini-Resolution
  - The compulsion sequence collapses; Voss and Harlan are detained by BMA channels.
  - Fog Hollow begins recovery as Isolde cooperates and Gus/Tomo begin re-warding; family prepares to leave with intact bonds.
  - Harry experiences the closing emotional beat: San Francisco is no longer a place he visits, but a place that once accepted him.
- Required character beats
  - Harry: refusal under coercion and emotional growth into belonging.
  - Voss/Isolde: final moral and tactical pivot on antagonist axis.
  - Molly: unconditional protection and forgiveness.
  - Arthur and Bill: family and strategy hold even while wounded.
  - Ron/Ginny/Marisol/Fred/George: collective witness to the endgame and shared family-extended safety.
- Stakes progression
  - Start: terminal threat to entire Fog Hollow.
  - End: communal safety restored; consequences are finite and living.
